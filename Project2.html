Now that we have obtained estimates for α, β, γ, , and Σ we can use the empirical bayes estimator to estimate . To do this we can use the following equations.
Since θ has no natural scale we put it in terms of "space mountain standy minute units". The average unobserved attendance over the entire sample is 60 space mountain minutes. Using θ we can determine which points in time have the highest demand, this could be used to enhance staffing decisions or the number of tickets to sell at a given time. I found the busiest day of the year to be December 31st, 2017 at 2:00PM. This makes sense intuitively since it is a holiday season where more people have time off work to go to Disneyland.
y_i = bsxfun(@minus, Y, (estMLE.a + estMLE.b*estMLE.gam)');
S = estMLE.b*estMLE.delta2*estMLE.b' + diag(estMLE.sigma2);
K = estMLE.delta2*estMLE.b'*(S \ eye(size(S)));
theta_hat = (estMLE.gam + (K*y_i'))';

Extension

Now we want to extend our model to more rides and allow for a different for different groups. In other words we want to be able to estimate the unobserved attendance for ride j among a group of teenagers, a group of nostalgic Disney fans, and a group of young families rather than a general estimate of unobserved attendance that combines all of these groups. In this example there will be 11 rides, SpaceMtn, BigThunMtn, SplashMtn, JungleCruise, HauntedMansion, Pirates, PeterPan, WinniePooh, SevenDwarfs, BuzzLight, and UnderSea.
